Why the council, not the school? In Scotland, state schools have no separate legal personality from their local authority. Under the Education (Scotland) Act 1980, the council acts as “education authority” and is the body that holds, decides on the use of, and controls pupil data. The school operationally delivers; the council legally controls. A DSA needs a legal person on each side, so the DSA is with the council.

The Yes path is often a no-op. If the existing DSA’s Schedule Part 8 is drafted as a standing description (e.g. “all [Council] secondary schools where MCR is delivering”), a new school is automatically covered the moment MCR begins delivering there. A variation letter is only needed when Schedule Part 8 names schools individually.

The No path’s timing depends on the council. Some councils have a templated process for non-commercial data sharing with charities and turn it around in two or three weeks. Others route it through procurement and a DPIA and can take two or three months. The DSA Builder shortens this by giving the council a clean, in-house-drafted starting point rather than asking their legal team to redline a third-party template from scratch.
